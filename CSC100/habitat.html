Habitat

Red pandas are typically found living in trees in the temperate forests of the eastern Himalayas. Almost 50% of red panda habitat is in the Eastern Himalayas. Red pandas live up mountains preferring high altitude environments ranging 7200ft to 15700ft. They like to live in climates of 10 to 25°C.


Habitat Range

  • Qinling Mountains, China
  • Southern Tibet
  • Sikkim, India
  • Assam, India
  • Bhutan
  • Northern Mountains of Burma
  • South-Western China
  • Hengduan Mountains, Sichuan
  • Gongshan Mountains, Yunnan

Red Panda Fun Fact:


Red pandas are highly territorial.

Eating Habits

via GIPHY

Red pandas are primarily considered a herbivore but the occasional bug and insect inclusion in their diet causes red pandas to also be considered omnivores. Their diet consists of grasses, fruits, insects and grubs. Red pandas, of course, eat lots of bamboo. 95% of a red panda’s diet consists of bamboo. Bamboo does not have high nutritional value so red pandas must eat a lot in order to get the nutrients they need. Red pandas eat anywhere from 20-30% of their body weight each day. Unlike the giant panda, red pandas are selective about the parts of the bamboo they eat. Red pandas will only eat the leaf tips and tender shoots.
